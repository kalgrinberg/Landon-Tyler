The History of the 1970 Oldsmobile 442 W-30

The Oldsmobile 442 was one of the original muscle cars, debuting in 1964 as an option
package for the Cutlass. The name "442" originally stood for four-barrel carburetor,
four-speed manual transmission, and dual exhaust. By 1970, the 442 had become its own
model and reached the peak of its performance potential.

The 1970 442 was built on GM's refreshed A-body platform with flowing, curvaceous lines
that many consider the most attractive of any GM mid-size that year. The standard engine
was a 455 cubic-inch (7.5-liter) V8 producing 365 horsepower and 500 lb-ft of torque,
making it one of the most powerful engines available in any production car.

The W-30 option package transformed the 442 into something truly special. It included
select-fit engine components with tighter tolerances, a performance camshaft, special
cylinder heads with larger valves, an aluminum intake manifold, forced-air induction
through twin scoops mounted under the front bumper, and a low-restriction exhaust system.
The W-30 engine was also rated at 370 horsepower, though it was noticeably stronger than
the standard 455.

Additional W-30 features included a fiberglass inner hood (painted red on the underside
as a visual cue), lightweight deck lid components, and a performance-calibrated Turbo
Hydra-Matic 400 or Muncie four-speed manual transmission. The package also included a
performance rear axle with higher numerical gear ratios.

The 1970 W-30 442 could accelerate from 0-60 mph in about 5.7 seconds and cover the
quarter-mile in the low 14-second range in stock form. The forced-air induction system
was particularly effective at higher speeds, providing cooler, denser air to the engine.

Oldsmobile produced approximately 3,100 442 W-30 models for 1970, including a small
number of convertibles that are extremely rare today. The W-30 option continued through
1972 with the 455 engine before being discontinued. The 1970 W-30 442 remains a favorite
among Oldsmobile enthusiasts and is one of the most respected muscle cars of the era.
