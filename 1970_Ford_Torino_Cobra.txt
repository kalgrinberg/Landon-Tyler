The History of the 1970 Ford Torino Cobra

The 1970 Ford Torino Cobra represented Ford's entry in the mid-size muscle car battle,
competing directly against the Chevelle SS, GTO, and Road Runner. The Torino was completely
redesigned for 1970 with dramatic "Coke bottle" styling that featured sweeping fender lines,
a long hood, and a short rear deck.

The Torino Cobra was the performance flagship of the Torino lineup. It came standard with
the 429 cubic-inch Cobra Jet V8 engine producing 370 horsepower, a four-speed manual
transmission (or optional C6 automatic), competition suspension, and a functional Shaker
hood scoop that protruded through the hood and vibrated with the engine. Seven-inch-wide
wheels with F70x14 tires were standard.

For those seeking even more power, the 429 Super Cobra Jet (SCJ) was available, adding
forged pistons, a solid-lifter camshaft, stronger connecting rods, and a mechanical fuel
pump. The SCJ was rated at the same 375 horsepower as the CJ, but its true output was
considerably higher. The Drag Pack option, which included the SCJ engine, was designed
specifically for drag racing and added a Detroit Locker rear differential with either
3.91:1 or 4.30:1 gears.

The Torino's new styling was not just attractive but also aerodynamically efficient. The
fastback SportsRoof body was derived from Ford's NASCAR program, and the Torino dominated
superspeedway racing in 1969 and 1970. Drivers like David Pearson and Cale Yarborough
scored numerous victories in Torino-bodied stock cars.

On the street, the 429 CJ Torino Cobra could sprint from 0-60 mph in approximately 5.8
seconds and cover the quarter-mile in the mid-13-second range. The car's substantial
3,700-pound curb weight was offset by the enormous torque of the 429 engine.

Ford produced approximately 7,675 Torino Cobras for 1970. While not as rare as some
competitors, the Torino Cobra's combination of NASCAR heritage, striking design, and
big-block power has earned it a well-deserved place among the greatest muscle cars of
the 1970s era. Collector values have risen steadily as enthusiasts recognize the Torino's
significance in Ford's performance history.
