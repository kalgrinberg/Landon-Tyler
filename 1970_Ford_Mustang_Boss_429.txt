The History of the 1970 Ford Mustang Boss 429

The Ford Mustang Boss 429 was born out of NASCAR necessity. Ford needed to homologate its
new 429 cubic-inch Semi-Hemi engine for use in NASCAR competition, which required that a
minimum number of production vehicles be equipped with the engine. The problem was that
the massive 429 engine would not fit in the standard Mustang engine bay without significant
modifications.

Ford contracted Kar Kraft, a specialty shop in Brighton, Michigan, to handle the
conversions. Each Boss 429 started as a standard Mustang SportsRoof (fastback) body that
was shipped to Kar Kraft, where the shock towers were moved outward, the inner fender wells
were modified, and the battery was relocated to the trunk to accommodate the enormous engine.

The 429 cubic-inch engine featured aluminum Semi-Hemi (crescent-shaped) combustion chambers,
a forged steel crankshaft, four-bolt main bearing caps, and a single 735-cfm Holley
four-barrel carburetor. Ford rated the engine at a very conservative 375 horsepower, though
actual output was believed to be well over 400 horsepower.

Each Boss 429 received a close-ratio four-speed manual transmission, a 3.91:1 rear axle
with a Traction-Lok differential, power front disc brakes, power steering, and a heavy-duty
cooling system. The cars were available in only a handful of colors, with most painted
Grabber Blue, Black Jade, Candy Apple Red, or Wimbledon White.

Ford produced 859 Boss 429 Mustangs for 1969 and 499 for 1970, for a total of 1,358 units
across both model years. The 1970 models received a revised front fascia, different
scoops, and other minor changes.

Ironically, the Boss 429 was not the quickest Mustang in a straight line due to its
NASCAR-oriented tuning and relatively mild street camshaft. The smaller Boss 302 was often
quicker in everyday driving. But the Boss 429's rarity, engineering significance, and
connection to Ford's racing program have made it one of the most valuable Mustangs ever
built, with pristine examples exceeding $400,000 at auction.
